Nikola Tesla

The father of wireless power

Here's some of many inventions by Nikola Tesla:

  • 1889 - Tesla Coil, Tesla fixed a Ruhmkorff coil by adding an air gap instead of insulating material between the primary and secondary windings and an iron core that could be moved to different positions in or out of the coil.
  • 1890 - Wireless Lighting, Tesla experimented with transmitting power by inductive and capacitive coupling using high AC voltages generated with his Tesla coil. He attempted to develop a wireless lighting system based on near-field inductive and capacitive coupling and conducted a series of public demonstrations where he lit Geissler tubes and even incandescent light bulbs from across a stage.
  • 1906 - Bladeless Turbine,Tesla demonstrated a 200 horsepower (150 kilowatts) 16,000 rpm bladeless turbine. During 1910–1911, at the Waterside Power Station in New York, several of his bladeless turbine engines were tested at 100–5,000 hp.

Tesla won numerous medals and awards over this time. Some of them include:

  • Serbia, 1892 - Grand Officer of the Order of St. Sava
  • Franklin Institute, US, 1894 - Elliott Cresson Medal
  • Montenegro, 1895 - Grand Cross of the Order of Prince Danilo I
  • US, 1896 - Member of the American Philosophical Society
  • Yugoslavia, 1926 - Grand Cross of the Order of St. Sava
  • Paris, France, 1937 - Medal of the University of Paris
